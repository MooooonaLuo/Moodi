MOODI - A SIMPLE MOOD TRACKER

Crafting Interaction Case Study • 2022

OVERVIEW

Moodi is a simple mood tracking app for people with depression or anxiety to understand their emotions better. Users can easily keep a journal of their moods daily and identify patterns and triggers. Unlike most of existing mood trackers, Moodi allows users to customize mood tags and has a virtual buddy to company them and give them advice.

Problem statement

How might we help people who deal with depression and anxiety to track their moods easily and provide them with informative and useful insights?

User research on reddit

Since mental health is a sensitive topic, people might feel uncomfortable talking to me in person. Therefore, I dived into Reddit to listen to people secretly to understand how people use mood trackers and their gains and pains. Many Reddit users greatly shared their experience and tips with various mood trackers they used to help out each other.

Many people share their tips and experience on Reddit

Key Insights

  • Mood trackers help people reflect how they feel overall in one day, track how long they have been feeling in a certain way, and identify patterns and triggers.
  • People don't like recording their mood when feeling down.
  • For those whose emotions fluctuate in one day, being able to record their emotions multiple times is necessary.
  • Some don't like typing, while some prefer adding texts to record their emotions more accurately.

Key Insights

  • More research is needed to determine the interaction with the digital character. The user hoped to talk to the character casually instead of being told about what to do or others' stories.
  • Confused about the mechanism of adding a new mood as well as the levels of pleasure and activation.
  • Year In Pixel might upset users by showing negative emotion only.
  • Analytics are interesting. People would love to see year in review about themselves.
